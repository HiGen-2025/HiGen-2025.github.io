2nd Workshop on

Human-Interactive Generation and Editing

HiGen @ CVPR 2026

June 3 - 7, 2026 | Denver, Colorado

About

The rapid evolution of generative AI has reshaped content creation across images, video, and 3D/4D visuals. This workshop focuses on cutting-edge methodologies, practical applications, and open challenges in image/video/3D/4D generation and related editing tasks with an emphasis on flexible and friendly human interactions and multi-modal control signals. This workshop will serve as a platform for researchers and practitioners to discuss key topics related to visual content creation and editing with versatile interactions.

Call for Papers

Topics

Topics of interests include, but are not limited to:

  • Interactive content creation and editing
  • Controllable generation with multi-modal interaction
  • Cross-domain cooperation for visual generative models
  • Generative world exploration and human object interaction
  • Foundational designs (pipeline, model, operator, etc.) for AIGC

Submission Guidelines

  • All papers must be submitted via the CVPR 2026 OpenReview workshop website.
  • Submission should be PDF format and follow the official CVPR 2026 template and guidelines.
  • Authors may upload optional supplementary materials, containing additional details, videos, images, etc.
  • Papers should follow the CVPR 2026 author guidelines and be anonymized for double-blind review.
  • The accepted papers will be presented as oral, or poster presentations.
  • The presentation of accepted papers at our workshop will follow the same policy as that for the accepted papers at the CVPR 2026 leading conference.
  • One Best Paper Award ($700) and one Runner-up Award ($300) will be granted to the most outstanding contributions, selected based on originality, technical merit, and potential impact from the accepted papers.

Submission Track 1: Proceedings Track

Submissions are expected to present original, unpublished work, and should be longer than 4 pages in length (up to 8 pages, including figures and tables but excluding references). Accepted papers will be published in the official CVPR workshop proceedings.

Important Dates (All deadlines are 23:59 AOE)

  • Paper Submission Deadline: March 15, 2026
  • Author Notification of Acceptance: March 21, 2026
  • Camera-ready Submission: April 10, 2026
  • Workshop Date: June 3-4, 2026
Submission Portal: Proceedings

Submission Track 2: Non-Proceedings Track

The Non-Proceedings Track offers a more flexible avenue for presenting a wider range of contributions without page limits. Accepted papers will NOT be published in the official CVPR workshop proceedings, but they can be presented and promoted as oral or poster presentations at our workshop. Submissions include but are not limited to:

  • Extended abstracts and short papers: We welcome submissions of up to 4 pages that describe work still in progress that is valuable to the community, or articulate insightful position papers on relevant topics.
  • Previously published work: We also accept submissions of work that has already been published elsewhere. This includes papers that may have been accepted at the main CVPR 2026 conference itself.

Important Dates (All deadlines are 23:59 AOE)

  • Author Notification of Acceptance: May 3, 2026
  • Camera-ready Submission: May 10, 2026
  • Workshop Date: June 3-4, 2026
Submission Portal: Non-proceedings
